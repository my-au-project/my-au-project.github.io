Initial Quote and Design Options

16 January 2026 · Preconstruction

Key takeaways

  • We discussed our requirements, preferred designs, and requested pricing.
  • The consultant provided a formal quote with multiple specification tiers.
  • We received floor plans and supporting documents to review.
  • We decided to move forward and book an in-office appointment.

Following our phone meeting with the consultant, we went through what we were looking for in a home, the designs we were interested in, and asked for price guidance to help us understand our options.

Later that day, the consultant sent through pricing along with supporting documentation. This included a PDF copy of the floor plan, the different specification tiers, and other related documents to review in our own time.

The pricing was based on the Swerve 12.5 design for our land with titles expected around March - May 2026.

The quote noted that pricing did not include any future changes to the plan or any required design guideline adjustments, should those be needed later.

Pricing options provided

  • Make it Count: [price] includes house, Noise B (provisional sum), siteworks (fixed), and BAL 12.5 (fixed).
  • Make it Bold: [price] includes house, Noise B (provisional sum), siteworks (fixed), and BAL 12.5 (fixed).
  • Make it Shout: [price] includes house, Noise B (provisional sum), siteworks (fixed), and BAL 12.5 (fixed).

Overall, we were happy with the information provided and felt comfortable progressing to the next step. We asked to book an office appointment to go through more details about the company, inclusions, pricing structure, and expected timelines.
