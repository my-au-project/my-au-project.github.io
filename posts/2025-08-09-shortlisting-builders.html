Shortlisting Builders

09 August 2025 · Build

Key takeaways

  • Research builders early, especially if it’s your first build.
  • Online reviews and community feedback are valuable but should be balanced.
  • Communication was our top priority when choosing a builder.
  • Inclusions, pricing, and timelines vary significantly between builders.
  • Visiting display homes helped us narrow our shortlist.

Since we hadn’t built a home before, choosing the right builder felt like one of the most important decisions we would make. We didn’t have prior experience to rely on, so we started by doing as much research as we could.

Our research process

We spent a lot of time reading through other people’s experiences online. This included browsing forums, reviews, and community discussions to get a sense of how different builders operated once contracts were signed.

  • Reddit discussions
  • Facebook groups and comments
  • ProductReview.com.au

While we took individual comments with a grain of salt, patterns started to emerge — especially around communication, delays, and build quality.

What we were looking for in a builder

To help narrow things down, we listed our key requirements and compared builders against them.

  • Communication
    Our number one priority. We wanted a builder that would keep us informed throughout the build and clearly communicate any changes.
  • Building inclusions
    Builders offer very different base inclusions. We compared what was included and what would cost extra.
  • Price
    The build needed to stay within our budget without compromising on quality.
  • Floor plans
    Our land could fit a 12.5m frontage, so we wanted a good range of designs that suited our lifestyle.
  • Admin timeframe
    How long planning, design, and approvals would take before construction started.
  • Build time
    Estimated time from slab down to handover.

Narrowing it down

After comparing all our options, we shortlisted four builders that best aligned with our priorities.

Our next step was to visit their display homes to get a better feel for the build quality, materials used, layouts, and overall finish.

Display homes helped us visualise layouts and finishes far better than brochures or floor plans ever could.

Walking through the homes gave us a clearer idea of room sizes, natural light, storage, and how the spaces would actually feel day to day.
