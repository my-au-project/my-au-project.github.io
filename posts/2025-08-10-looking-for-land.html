Looking for Land

10 August 2025 · Land

Key takeaways

  • Start looking for land as soon as your borrowing capacity is clear.
  • Use both developer websites and land marketplaces.
  • Shortlist blocks early to compare true costs.
  • Price per square metre matters more than headline price.
  • Hidden factors like BAL, noise, and soil can impact build costs.

Once we had a clear idea of how much we could borrow, the next step was finding the right block of land. This stage required more research and patience than we initially expected.

Where we looked

There are many websites where you can check land availability and pricing. Major developers usually have their own platforms showing current and upcoming estates.

  • Developer websites (for example, Satterley)
  • Land marketplaces such as wegotland.com.au
  • Home builders that offer land-finding services

Shortlisting our options

After identifying several blocks we liked, we listed land options across the areas we were genuinely considering living in. From there, we created a shortlist so we could compare them side by side rather than making decisions in isolation.

Our shortlisting criteria included:

  • Amenities available (schools, shops, parks, transport)
  • Distance to work and overall commute time
  • Land size and how usable the block would be
  • Price per square metre, not just the total land price
  • Additional factors that could increase build costs, such as:
    • BAL (Bushfire Attack Level) ratings
    • Noise ratings
    • Soil classifications

Some blocks looked appealing at first, but once we considered these additional requirements, they became less attractive due to the potential impact on our overall build budget.

Taking the time to properly shortlist helped us avoid surprises later and made the final decision feel far more confident.

Working with a builder

We initially asked a home consultant to help us secure land. While this option was convenient, the process wasn’t as quick as we wanted it to be.

Land can move quickly in popular estates, so timing and responsiveness matter.

Contacting the developer directly

To speed things up, we contacted the developer ourselves to inquire about availability. This gave us clearer answers and allowed us to act quickly when the right block became available.

Once we found a lot we were happy with, we were able to place a hold on the land by paying a deposit, which gave us confidence to move forward.
