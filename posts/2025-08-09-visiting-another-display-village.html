Visiting Another Display Village

09 August 2025 · Builder Research

Key takeaways

  • Visiting multiple display villages helps compare builders more effectively.
  • Seeing different frontages and layouts in person is invaluable.
  • Preparation leads to more meaningful conversations with builders.
  • Knowing your budget and floor plans saves time for both you and the consultant.
  • Display villages are best used for comparison, not commitment.

On 09 August 2025, we decided to visit another display village, this time north of Perth at the Illyarrie estate .

The goal of this visit was simple: look at what was available in this display village and compare how different builders approach their designs, layouts, and inclusions.

Exploring different builders and frontages

One thing we really liked about this visit was being able to see homes with different frontages. Walking through multiple homes back-to-back helped us understand how the same block width can feel very different depending on the design.

We visited each of our shortlisted builders and also looked at display homes from Celebration Homes and Home Group. Seeing a variety of builders in one place made comparisons far easier than visiting them individually.

Seeing floor plans in real life

While floor plans on paper are useful, nothing compares to seeing room sizes in person. This visit helped us better understand:

  • How different layouts affect flow through the home
  • Actual room sizes compared to what they look like on plans
  • How ceiling heights change the overall feel of a space

Walking through the homes gave us a much clearer idea of what would work for our lifestyle and what wouldn’t.

Observing other buyers

While walking through the display village, we noticed a few other visitors speaking with consultants and asking questions about house and land pricing.

Many of them didn’t seem particularly prepared. They often had no clear idea of their budget, which areas they were interested in, how large a block they wanted, or whether they had finance in place.

From our perspective, these conversations didn’t seem very productive. It reinforced how important preparation is before engaging with builders, especially when discussing pricing and feasibility.

How we approached the conversation

When we spoke with consultants, we took a different approach. We explained that:

  • We had already spoken with our mortgage broker
  • We were in the process of obtaining our Letter of Eligibility (LOE)
  • We had a shortlist of floor plans we were interested in

We presented the floor plans we liked and asked specific questions around:

  • Pricing for each plan
  • The cost of potential changes and upgrades
  • What building inclusions were standard
  • Estimated administration and pre-construction time
  • Typical construction time from slab to handover

Being prepared made a noticeable difference. The conversations were more detailed, practical, and tailored to our situation rather than generic.

What we took away from the visit

This visit helped validate some of our earlier assumptions and also raised new questions we hadn’t considered before. Seeing different builders side by side made it easier to narrow down what mattered most to us.

Overall, it was another valuable step in our build journey and helped us feel more confident moving forward.
