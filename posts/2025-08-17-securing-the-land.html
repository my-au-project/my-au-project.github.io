Securing the Land

17 August 2025 · Land

Key takeaways

  • A Letter of Eligibility (LOE) was required to show borrowing capacity.
  • The developer requested ID and full legal details for the contract.
  • The land contract was issued and signed digitally via DocuSign.
  • A deposit was transferred once the seller accepted the contract.
  • We received the completed land contract after all admin was finalised.

After deciding on the block we wanted, the next step was officially securing the land. This was a major milestone in our journey and the point where everything started to feel real.

Providing our Letter of Eligibility (LOE)

The first document the developer asked for was our Letter of Eligibility (LOE). This shows our potential borrowing power and gives the developer confidence that we are financially capable of proceeding with the purchase.

While it isn’t a formal loan approval, it was sufficient for the developer to move forward with preparing the land contract.

Information requested by the developer

To prepare the land contract, the developer’s sales consultant asked us to provide several details and documents.

  • Passport or driver’s licence for identification
  • Full legal names of all buyers (as per ID)
  • Current residential address
  • Email addresses for all buyers
  • Mobile numbers for all buyers
  • Confirmation of the lot number we wished to purchase

Once all information was confirmed, the contract preparation process began.

Signing the land contract

The sales consultant sent us the land contract electronically via DocuSign. This made the process straightforward and allowed us to review and sign the documents without needing to attend an office.

We were advised that once the seller accepted the contract, the next step would be transferring the deposit to formally secure the land.

Paying the deposit

After receiving confirmation that the seller had accepted the contract, we arranged the deposit transfer as instructed. This step officially secured the land and removed it from the market.

Paying the deposit was the moment it truly felt locked in.

Contract completed

After all administrative checks and processing were completed, we received the fully executed land contract. At this point, the land was officially secured, subject to settlement.

With the land locked in, we could confidently move forward with builder selection and detailed design, knowing we had a solid foundation in place.
