First Display Home Appointment

07 August 2025 · Builder Research

Key takeaways

  • Display villages usually host multiple builders, not just one.
  • Your first appointment is about learning the process, not committing.
  • A Letter of Eligibility (LOE) is critical when securing land.
  • Coming prepared with floor plans helps speed up quoting.
  • Seeing finishes in person makes inclusions much easier to understand.

On 07 August 2025, we booked our very first display home appointment with Blueprint Homes at the new Treeby display village, located in the Treeby estate .

Since this was our first ever builder appointment, we went in with one main goal: learn as much as possible about the building process.

Learning about display villages

One of the first things we learned was that a display home is usually part of a display village. That means multiple builders each have their own display homes within the same estate, which makes it easier to compare designs, inclusions, and build quality in one visit.

Meeting the consultant

We met our Blueprint Homes consultant and walked through the entire build journey, from start to finish. They provided us with a starter pack that covered a wide range of topics, including:

  • Finance and loan considerations
  • Pre-construction and planning stages
  • The construction process
  • Finding and securing land
  • Site works and potential costs
  • Building inclusions
  • First home buyer benefits and grants

The importance of a Letter of Eligibility (LOE)

A Letter of Eligibility (LOE) is essential when placing a deposit on land. Without it, developers are unlikely to take you seriously.

The consultant emphasised that when you’re ready to secure land, you’ll need a Letter of Eligibility (LOE). This letter confirms that you have the borrowing capacity to finance the land purchase.

Apparently, without an LOE, developers may not even proceed with discussions. Fortunately for us, we were already in the process of obtaining one from the very beginning.

Discussing floor plans and pricing

We already had a shortlist of floor plans we liked, so we shared those with the consultant and asked for rough pricing. Our aim was to understand:

  • Base build cost
  • Estimated site works
  • Additional upgrades we were considering

Some of the upgrades we asked about included painting, 31c ceiling heights throughout the home, and other commonly upgraded inclusions.

Walking through the display home

After the discussion, the consultant walked us through the display home and pointed out key features such as:

  • Ceiling heights and overall spatial feel
  • Kitchen layout and flow
  • Stone benchtops
  • Cabinetry finishes and storage solutions

Seeing these elements in person helped us better understand what inclusions actually look and feel like, rather than just reading them on a brochure.

After the appointment

We left the appointment feeling far more informed and confident about the process. It was reassuring to get real, practical information directly from a builder.

The following day, the consultant emailed us detailed pricing for the floor plans we requested, including site works and the additional upgrades we had discussed.

This gave us our first realistic idea of what building could actually cost and helped set expectations for the next stages of our journey.
