Appointment with Celebration Homes

20 August 2025 · Builder Appointment

Key takeaways

  • Builder office appointments feel very different to display home visits.
  • Being clear on finance and land helps focus the discussion on the builder.
  • Seeing materials in a showroom makes inclusions easier to understand.
  • Local WA-based trades can allow more design flexibility.
  • This meeting helped us assess overall fit, not just price.

On 20 August 2025, we had our first appointment with Celebration Homes. This meeting felt quite different from our previous display home visits, as it took place at their office in Leederville.

Meeting details

  • Date: Wednesday, 20 August 2025
  • Time: 6:00 PM
  • Location: 301 Vincent Street, Leederville WA 6007

Prior to the meeting, Celebration Homes sent us an outline of what would be covered. This helped set expectations and made the session feel structured and purposeful.

What the meeting covered

The discussion focused on:

  • Understanding what we were looking for in a new home
  • Reviewing timelines and the overall building process
  • Walking us through the Celebration Homes experience and support provided
  • Discussing initial pricing estimates for different designs and land
  • A tour of their Home Collective showroom to showcase specifications and features

We were encouraged to bring any ideas, documents, or questions, which made the meeting feel collaborative rather than sales-driven.

A different type of appointment

Compared to display home visits, this appointment felt more focused and personal. Being in their office environment allowed for a deeper discussion about how Celebration Homes operates as a builder.

Because we already had our finance and land sorted, we were able to focus entirely on understanding the builder itself — their process, inclusions, and whether they would be the right fit for us.

Touring the Home Collective showroom

One of the highlights of the appointment was walking through their Home Collective showroom. This space allows you to see and feel the actual materials used in their builds.

We were able to view and touch:

  • Benchtop materials
  • Cabinetry finishes
  • Cooktops and appliances
  • Tiles
  • Doors and internal finishes

Being able to physically see and feel these materials made it much easier to understand what was included and how different options might look in a finished home.

What stood out

One thing that really stood out was the level of customisation available, supported by WA-based contractors.

A key takeaway from this meeting was learning that Celebration Homes can customise certain designs more easily. Many of their contractors are WA-based, and they manufacture some components locally, such as cabinetry.

This flexibility made the builder more appealing, as it opened up more options for tailoring a home to suit our needs rather than being locked into a rigid design.

After the appointment

By the end of the meeting, we had a much clearer understanding of what Celebration Homes could offer and how they might fit into our overall build journey.

It was a productive session that helped us compare builders beyond just pricing and floor plans, and instead focus on process, quality, and flexibility.
