Appointment with Home Group

29 August 2025 · Builder Appointment

Key takeaways

  • Sending our expectations before the meeting leads to a more productive discussion.
  • Sharing LOE and land details helps get realistic pricing early.
  • Showrooms help visualise finishes and promotional upgrades.
  • Customisation depends heavily on how and where materials are manufactured.
  • Understanding what is standard vs custom avoids cost surprises.

On 29 August 2025, we had an appointment with Home Group. Learning from our previous builder appointments, we decided to prepare thoroughly before this meeting to ensure the discussion would be focused and productive.

Preparing before the meeting

Prior to the appointment, we sent Home Group a detailed email outlining our situation, intentions, and expectations. This allowed them to prepare pricing and information specific to our build rather than giving generic answers.

At this stage, we had already received our Letter of Eligibility (LOE) and secured a block of land, with the title expected between March and April 2026.

Land requirements shared

  • BAL 12.5
  • Noise Package B
  • Soil Class S

We asked Home Group to provide estimates on how these requirements would impact the overall build cost.

Design and pricing requests

We were particularly interested in the Maldives design and asked for a cost breakdown across:

  • Standard specifications
  • Designer Plus specifications
  • Luxury specifications

We also requested quotes for the following upgrades:

  • Wall painting
  • Vinyl flooring to all rooms (no carpets)
  • Additional 30 power points
  • Additional 30 LED lights

Questions about promotions

During a previous visit to a display home in Treeby, we noticed several promotional upgrades and wanted more clarity around them.

  • Aluminium front door and electronic smart lock
    – How long does the promotion run?
  • Solar panels
    – Brand, panel size, battery inclusion, warranty, and specifications

Booking the appointment

To make scheduling easy, we shared our availability upfront:

  • Friday, 29 August 2025 – from 5:00 PM
  • Wednesday, 3 September 2025 – from 5:00 PM

This approach allowed Home Group to prepare in advance, which made the discussion far smoother once we arrived.

Visiting the Home Group office and showroom

When we arrived at the Home Group office, we were impressed by their showroom, particularly the kitchens, which helped us better understand the finishes, inclusions, and promotional upgrades.

Discussing the build process

During the meeting, we went through:

  • Their overall build process
  • Standard inclusions and upgrade options
  • Current promotions
  • Price breakdowns

We also discussed potential customisations, particularly around kitchen design and layout.

What we learned about customisation

Customisation costs depend heavily on where materials are manufactured.

One important thing we learned was that some of Home Group’s materials and products are manufactured outside Australia. Because of this, certain designs come as standard.

Customising these elements can cost extra, as changes may need to be manufactured locally rather than coming from their standard supply chain. This was an important insight when weighing flexibility versus cost.

Overall impression

This appointment felt very efficient and informative. Preparing ahead of time clearly paid off, as the discussion was tailored to our situation and focused on real numbers rather than assumptions.

By the end of the meeting, we had a clearer understanding of Home Group’s offerings, strengths, and limitations, which helped us further narrow down our builder shortlist.
