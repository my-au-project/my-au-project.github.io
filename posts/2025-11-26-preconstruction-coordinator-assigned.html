Allocated a Customer Experience Coordinator

26 November 2025 · Pre-construction

Key takeaways

  • It took almost three months before we were officially assigned a pre-construction coordinator.
  • The pre-construction stage covers contracts, approvals, and preparation before building starts.
  • Having a dedicated coordinator clarified what to expect next.
  • Most major consultations only happen after the HIA contract is signed.

On this date, we were finally allocated a Customer Experience Coordinator for our pre-construction journey. By this point, it had been almost three months since we paid our deposit.

This was the first time we had a clearly defined point of contact whose role was specifically focused on guiding us through the pre-construction phase.

What pre-construction involves

The coordinator explained that pre-construction is the stage where all the groundwork is completed to prepare the home for construction. This includes:

  • Finalising the building contracts
  • Securing council approvals
  • Preparing documents, drawings, and specifications for the site

While construction itself is the most visible stage, this phase is where many of the critical decisions and administrative tasks happen behind the scenes.

Ongoing communication and support

The coordinator outlined her role as keeping us informed, guiding us through upcoming steps, and answering questions as they arise. She also mentioned that she would check in with us on a fortnightly basis to provide updates and keep us involved.

We were asked to confirm our preferred communication method via phone, email, or a combination of both to make sure updates worked around our availability.

Upcoming consultations

During pre-construction, we were advised to expect several key consultations:

  • Design Brief (1.5 hours) — A session with an expert consultant to discuss our vision, style, and options, guided through the selection centre.
  • Electrical & Technology Consultation (2 hours) — Covering electrical layout, smart technology, air conditioning, and solar selections. This can be booked once the HIA contract is signed.
  • Selections Consultation — The final product and colour selection meeting at Home Collective. Five hours are allocated, but it’s recommended to keep the whole day free. This is also booked after signing the HIA contract.

Travel plans and timing

The coordinator acknowledged that we would be out of the country from the end of February through all of March and asked us to flag any finance-related deadlines that might be affected by this timing.

We were also advised that additional information about what to expect during pre-construction would be uploaded to the Building Hub.

Our thoughts at this stage

Having a dedicated coordinator brought some clarity to what had previously felt like a slow and uncertain phase. While there was still a lot to be done, it helped to finally understand the structure of pre-construction and what steps were meant to come next.

At this point, we were hopeful that progress would start to feel more tangible with clearer communication and defined milestones.
