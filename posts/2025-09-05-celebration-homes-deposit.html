Paying the Build Deposit

05 September 2025 · Builder

Key takeaways

  • Having land and finance sorted allowed us to move quickly into pre-construction.
  • Itemised costing gave us clarity and control over our build budget.
  • Paying the deposit starts the pre-construction process.
  • Always ask for signed copies of contracts and documents.
  • Builder processes and follow-through matter just as much as inclusions.

This meeting with Celebration Homes was an important one for us. The goal was to go through the design, review the itemised costs, and pay the deposit to start the pre-construction process.

Why timing mattered

At this point, our situation was clear and well planned:

  • Land finance settlement expected in February 2026
  • Land titles estimated for March - April 2026
  • Overseas holiday planned for late February and all of March

Because of this, our goal was to complete pre-construction by mid-January to February 2026, so everything would be ready to go as soon as the land titled. Given it was only September, we felt we had plenty of time, especially when factoring in the December holiday period.

Design review and itemised costs

During the meeting, we revisited the floor plan changes we had previously discussed and went through the cost of each item in the pre-construction contract.

This was the first time we really understood what everything would cost, line by line. Having this visibility meant we could pick and choose what to include based on the budget we had allocated for the build.

It felt reassuring to finally have numbers instead of estimates and to make decisions with confidence.

Paying the deposit

After confirming that everything looked correct, we proceeded with paying the deposit to officially start the process.

We left the appointment feeling excited, knowing the next steps would involve selecting cabinetry styles, colours, flooring, and other finishes. It really felt like things were moving forward.

An early oversight

One thing we missed at the time was asking for copies of the contract and other documents we signed. We assumed these would be sent through via email afterward, which looking back was an oversight on our part.

Always ask for copies of signed documents before leaving the appointment.

What happened next

Unfortunately, Celebration Homes did not follow through with the process as expected. While we were anticipating progress through to February, nothing moved forward.

This ultimately led us to reassess our options and explore other builders. We cover this in a later post: 15 January 2026 - Search for new builder.
